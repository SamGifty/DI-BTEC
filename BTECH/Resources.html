Climate Change in Ghana

Ghana faces significant challenges from climate change, including rising temperatures, erratic rainfall, coastal erosion, and threats to agriculture. This website provides essential information, resources, and tools to help Ghanaians understand and respond to these challenges.

Key Climate Impacts in Ghana:

  • Temperature increase of 1-3Â°C projected by 2050
  • Sea level rise threatening coastal communities
  • Declining cocoa yields in major growing regions
  • Increased frequency of extreme weather events
  • Pressure on water resources and hydroelectric power

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Ghana affected by climate change? +
Ghana experiences rising temperatures, erratic rainfall patterns, coastal erosion (especially in places like Keta), reduced agricultural productivity, and increased frequency of extreme weather events. The northern regions face desertification while coastal areas deal with flooding and erosion.
What are the main causes of climate change in Ghana? +
While global emissions drive climate change, Ghana contributes through deforestation (especially in the Ashanti and Eastern regions), illegal mining ("galamsey"), fossil fuel use, and agricultural practices. Energy production, land use changes, and transportation are major emission sectors.
What is Ghana doing to combat climate change? +
Ghana has implemented the National Climate Change Policy, participates in international agreements, promotes renewable energy (like the Bui Dam solar project), and has initiatives like the Ghana REDD+ Strategy to reduce deforestation. The country has also developed a National Adaptation Plan.
How does climate change affect food security in Ghana? +
Erratic rainfall affects crop yields, especially for staple crops like maize and yam. Rising temperatures also impact cocoa production, a key export crop. Fish stocks are declining due to warming ocean temperatures, affecting coastal communities' livelihoods and protein sources.
What can ordinary Ghanaians do to help? +
Planting trees, practicing sustainable farming, reducing waste, conserving energy, and supporting climate-friendly policies can all make a difference. Individuals can also educate others, participate in community adaptation projects, and choose sustainable transportation options when possible.
